Quick Answer

GST replies need return data and proof

A useful GST notice response normally compares GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, 2A/2B, e-way bills, invoices, payments, ledgers and portal notices. The right reply depends on the notice reason and due date.

Do not reply only with a short explanation. GST notices usually need transaction-level reconciliation and attachments.
When You Need This

Situations this page is built for

  • GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B turnover mismatch.
  • ITC mismatch with GSTR-2B or supplier data.
  • Late filing or tax demand notice.
  • Registration cancellation or suspension notice.
  • E-way bill, invoice or place-of-supply issue.
Keep Ready

Documents and details usually required

  • GST notice/order PDF and reference number.
  • GST login access or downloaded returns.
  • GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, 2A/2B and electronic ledgers.
  • Sales and purchase invoices.
  • E-way bill and e-invoice data where applicable.
  • Books of accounts and bank statements.
How It Works

Practical process before hiring

Read notice grounds

Identify section, period, due date, amount and portal action expected.

Reconcile returns

Compare outward supply, ITC, tax paid and ledger data period-wise.

Prepare attachments

Create concise workings and attach invoices, ledgers or explanations where required.

File reply and monitor

Submit through the GST portal and track personal hearing or further clarification.

Avoid Mistakes

Common red flags and mistakes

  • Replying without reconciling GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B.
  • Ignoring supplier ITC mismatches.
  • Missing the portal deadline.
  • Uploading raw invoices without summary workings.
  • Not checking interest, late fee and tax paid ledgers.
WorkIndex Request Checklist

What to mention when you post

  • GSTIN status, business type and the period/months involved.
  • Whether the need is registration, return filing, notice reply, cancellation or reconciliation.
  • Approximate invoice count and whether ecommerce/e-way bill/e-invoice data is involved.
  • Any due date, notice reference number or cancellation order date.
  • Whether books, invoices and GST login access are ready.
Hiring Criteria

How to choose the right professional

  • Choose someone who asks for return data before quoting complex work.
  • Check experience with GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, 2B reconciliation and portal replies.
  • Ask whether late fee, interest and tax payment calculations are included.
  • For notices, confirm that the expert will prepare a point-wise reply with attachments.
  • For monthly retainers, clarify filing calendar and document collection process.
FAQs

Questions people ask before hiring

Can a GST notice be resolved online?

Many replies can be filed online on the GST portal, but the exact route depends on notice type.

What is the first thing to check?

Check notice period, due date, tax amount, mismatch reason and whether personal hearing is offered.

Do I need a CA for GST notice?

Not always, but an experienced GST consultant helps when mismatch, ITC, demand or cancellation risk is involved.
