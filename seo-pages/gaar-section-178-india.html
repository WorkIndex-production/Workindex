Info service

What this covers

GAAR is aimed at arrangements where tax benefit is the main purpose and the structure lacks commercial substance or abuses the law. High-value restructurings need documented business purpose.

  • Section 178 and allied provisions should be read with commercial-substance tests and official guidance.
  • The commonly cited Rs. 3 crore threshold should be checked against current rules before relying on it.
  • GAAR is different from specific anti-avoidance rules and transfer pricing.
Use cases

Who this is for

  • Founder restructuring shareholding.
  • Group creating holding entities.
  • Company planning merger or acquisition.
  • HNI using offshore structures.
Records

Documents and data to verify

  • Transaction documents.
  • Board/business rationale.
  • Valuation and tax note.
  • Substance evidence.
  • Treaty documents if relevant.
Care points

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Structuring only for tax benefit.
  • No board-level commercial rationale.
  • Assuming treaty benefit is automatic.
  • Ignoring transfer pricing and FEMA alongside GAAR.
Action

How to proceed

  • Confirm the applicable year, taxpayer type, state, registration status and portal form before acting.
  • Reconcile the official portal data with books, bank statements, certificates, invoices and notices.
  • Prepare a written computation, filing note, document checklist or response with assumptions clearly stated.
  • Download acknowledgements, challans, workings and evidence after filing or submission.
