ESOP Taxation in India
Employee guide for exercise and sale

ESOP tax usually has two stages: perquisite tax at exercise and capital gains tax at sale. Listed, unlisted and foreign-parent shares need careful reporting.

ESOP taxation has two stages

StageTax treatmentKey document
ExerciseFMV minus exercise price is salary perquisite taxed at slab rateForm 16 and ESOP exercise statement
SaleSale price minus FMV at exercise is capital gainsBroker statement, buyback letter or company statement
Blog

Listed versus unlisted ESOPs

Listed company FMV is usually linked to exchange value. Unlisted company FMV may need merchant banker valuation. Holding period and rate depend on security type and law for that year.

Blog

Documents to collect

  • ESOP grant and exercise letter.
  • Form 16 showing perquisite value.
  • Broker statement or company buyback letter.
  • Merchant banker certificate for unlisted ESOPs.
  • AIS showing salary/perquisite and capital gains entries.
Blog

Common mistakes

  • Not reporting perquisite income.
  • Using exercise price instead of FMV at exercise as cost basis for sale.
  • Misclassifying unlisted share holding period.
  • Missing foreign asset reporting where ESOPs are overseas.

Questions People Ask

FAQs

Are ESOPs taxed when granted?

Generally tax arises at exercise and sale, not merely at grant, but plan terms matter.

What is ESOP cost for capital gains?

Commonly FMV considered for perquisite at exercise becomes cost reference for later sale.

Do foreign ESOPs need disclosure?

Foreign shares/assets can require foreign asset reporting by residents.
