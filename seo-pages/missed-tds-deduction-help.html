Late deduction, Section 201 demand and how to fix it

Problem help

Missing TDS deduction makes payer liable

If a business fails to deduct TDS, it may be treated as assessee in default under Section 201. Interest can apply for non-deduction and non-payment, and TRACES demand may follow.

Problem help

Situations this page is built for

  • Vendor or contractor paid without TDS.
  • Rent above threshold paid without TDS.
  • Professional fee paid without 194J deduction.
  • TRACES demand received later.
  • TDS deposited in wrong assessment year or quarter.
Problem help

Documents required

  • Original invoices and bank payments.
  • Vendor/consultant PAN.
  • TDS return for quarter.
  • TRACES demand if received.
  • Proof payee paid tax, if relying on certificate route.
Problem help

Process

Calculate interest

Work out interest under Section 201(1A).

Recover/deduct

Deduct from next payment or recover where possible.

Deposit challan

Pay TDS and interest correctly.

File correction return

Update TDS return and challan mapping.

Issue Form 16A

Provide certificate after filing.

Respond to demand

Close TRACES demand with correction or response.

Problem help

Common mistakes

  • Doing nothing after discovering missed TDS.
  • Deducting but not depositing.
  • Depositing in wrong AY/quarter.
  • Not obtaining payee tax-paid certificate where relevant.
