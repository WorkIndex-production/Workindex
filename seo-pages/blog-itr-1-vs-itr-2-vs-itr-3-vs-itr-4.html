Which ITR Form to File?
ITR-1 to ITR-4 explained clearly

Choosing the wrong ITR form can lead to defective returns, missed schedules and incorrect reporting of capital gains, business income or foreign assets.

Blog

ITR-1 Sahaj

Commonly used for eligible resident individuals with salary, one house property and other income within limits. Not for capital gains, foreign assets, directors or multiple-house cases.

Blog

ITR-2

Used where capital gains, multiple properties, foreign income/assets or director/shareholder conditions apply, but not business/professional income.

Blog

ITR-3

Used for business or professional income with books, partners in firms, F&O traders and more complex income reporting.

Blog

ITR-4 Sugam

Used for eligible presumptive taxation under 44AD, 44ADA or 44AE subject to conditions and limits.

Blog

Quick decision checklist

  • Salary plus interest only: check ITR-1 eligibility.
  • Capital gains or foreign income: consider ITR-2.
  • Business/professional income with books: ITR-3.
  • Eligible presumptive income: ITR-4.
Blog

Common form selection mistakes

  • Using ITR-1 after selling property.
  • Using ITR-4 with capital gains.
  • F&O trader using ITR-2.
  • Ignoring foreign assets or directorship restrictions.

Which ITR Form to File?: year and source check

Last fact-checked: 25 May 2026.

AY 2026-27 means FY 2025-26 income under the Income-tax Act, 1961. Tax Year 2026-27 means FY 2026-27 income under the Income Tax Act, 2025. Do not mix the two labels.

For AY 2026-27, check the ITR utility, validation rules and official e-filing guidance before relying on secondary summaries.

Use official portal pages, CBDT notifications, the supplied Act PDF and ICAI material before making a filing, payroll, TDS/TCS or rebate decision.

What to verify for Which ITR Form to File?

  • Correct financial year, assessment year or tax year.
  • Taxpayer type, age category, residential status and business/profession status.
  • Exact income heads, including salary, house property, business/profession, capital gains, VDA and other sources.

  • Deductions/exemptions allowed in the selected regime and current ITR utility validation rules.
