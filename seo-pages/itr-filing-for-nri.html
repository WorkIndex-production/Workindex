NRI ITR depends on status and income type

Residential status is determined by stay days and related rules for the relevant year. NRIs are generally taxed in India on income received, accrued or deemed to accrue in India, such as rent, Indian capital gains, NRO interest, India workdays salary and business income from India.

Situations this page is built for

  • NRI with rental income from Indian property.
  • NRI who sold Indian property.
  • NRO FD interest with high TDS.
  • Returned Indian with split-year facts.
  • DTAA claim for USA, UK, UAE, Singapore or other residence country.
  • Foreign tax credit or Form 67 review.

Documents required

  • Passport travel dates.
  • PAN and Indian bank details.
  • NRO/NRE statements and TDS certificates.
  • Rent agreement and property tax records.
  • Sale deed and cost documents for capital gains.
  • TRC and Form 10F for treaty claims where applicable.

Process

Determine status

Count days and check resident/NRI/RNOR impact.

Map income

Separate India-sourced income from foreign income.

Check DTAA

Use TRC/Form 10F where treaty benefit applies.

File correct ITR

Usually ITR-2 or ITR-3 depending on business/professional income.

Last fact-checked: 25 May 2026.

AY 2026-27 means FY 2025-26 income under the Income-tax Act, 1961. Tax Year 2026-27 means FY 2026-27 income under the Income Tax Act, 2025. Do not mix the two labels.

For AY 2026-27, check the ITR utility, validation rules and official e-filing guidance before relying on secondary summaries.

Use official portal pages, CBDT notifications, the supplied Act PDF and ICAI material before making a filing, payroll, TDS/TCS or rebate decision.
