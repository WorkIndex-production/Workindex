Commercial versus residential rent

Commercial property rent is generally taxable at 18% where registration applies. Residential rent to an individual for personal residence is generally exempt, while residential property rented to a GST-registered person can trigger RCM rules after the 2022 change.

Blog

Post-2022 residential rent RCM rule

If a GST-registered person rents residential property, the recipient may need to pay GST under reverse charge depending on facts. This often affects companies taking flats for employees or business use.

Blog

Practical scenarios

  • Company renting flat for employee accommodation: RCM review needed.
  • Individual renting flat to individual for residence: generally exempt.
  • Co-working or commercial office rental: taxable if threshold/registration applies.
  • Warehouse rental: commercial treatment usually applies.

Documents landlord needs

  • Rent agreement.
  • GST registration if commercial/taxable rent crosses threshold.
  • Bank statements for rent credit.
  • TDS certificate from tenant where applicable.
  • Property ownership and municipal tax records.
