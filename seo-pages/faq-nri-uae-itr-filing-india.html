Fact check

Accuracy notes before you act

  • Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) u/s 115JB applies to companies at 15% of book profits if normal corporate tax liability is lower.
  • Secretarial Audit in Form MR-3 is mandatory under Section 204 for listed companies, large public companies, and companies with bank debt > ₹100 crore.
  • Income of a spouse or minor child from assets gifted without adequate consideration is clubbed with the transferor's income under Section 64.
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) must file ITR-2 or ITR-3 in India to declare Indian-sourced income (interest, capital gains, rental) and can claim DTAA relief u/s 90.

Questions People Ask

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who is considered a Non-Resident Indian (NRI) for tax purposes?

You are an NRI if you stay in India for less than 182 days in the FY, or less than 60 days in the FY plus less than 365 days in the preceding 4 years.

2. Is an NRI taxed on their global income in India?

No. NRIs are only taxed on income earned, accrued, or received in India. Global income earned outside India is not taxable in India.

3. Which ITR form should an NRI file for FY 2025-26?

NRIs must file ITR-2 (if they have salary, property, or capital gains) or ITR-3 (if they have business income). NRIs cannot file ITR-1.

4. Is the Section 87A tax rebate available to NRIs?

No. The Section 87A rebate (which makes tax zero up to ₹12 lakh under the New Regime) is only available to resident individuals. NRIs do not qualify.

5. How is NRE and NRO bank account interest taxed for NRIs?

Interest earned on NRE (Non-Resident External) accounts is fully tax-free in India. Interest on NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) accounts is taxable and subject to TDS.

6. Can an NRI claim benefits under Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements (DTAA)?

Yes, NRIs can claim lower tax rates or exemptions under DTAA by submitting a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) and Form 10F.

7. What is the TDS rate on payments made to NRIs?

TDS under Section 195 on payments to NRIs is deducted at the maximum marginal rate (e.g. 30% on rental income or short-term gains) unless a lower tax certificate is obtained.

8. Can an NRI invest in mutual funds or real estate in India? How is it taxed?

Yes, NRIs can invest. Capital gains are taxed at 12.5% for LTCG (equity/property) and standard rates for STCG, with TDS deducted at source.

9. What is the deadline for filing ITR for NRIs?

The due date is July 31, 2026 (or October 31 if subject to audit).

10. Do NRIs have to report foreign bank accounts in ITR?

NRIs do not need to report foreign bank accounts or assets in Schedule FA. Only resident taxpayers are mandatory to report foreign assets.

11. How can I track my Income Tax Refund status?

Log in to the e-filing portal and go to e-File > Income Tax Returns > View Filed Returns to check your status. Ensure your bank account is pre-validated on the e-filing portal and linked with Aadhaar/PAN to receive the refund directly.

12. Is Aadhaar mandatory for NRIs filing ITR?

Aadhaar is not mandatory for NRIs who do not possess one. They can file using their PAN.

13. Can I file a revised return to correct mistakes in my ITR? What is the deadline?

Yes, under Section 139(5), you can file a revised return to correct any errors or omissions in your original filing. The deadline to file a revised return for FY 2025-26 is March 31, 2027.

14. What is an Updated Return (ITR-U)? When can it be filed?

Under Section 139(8A), you can file an Updated Return (ITR-U) within 24 months from the end of the relevant Assessment Year (for FY 2025-26, until March 31, 2029) to declare additional income. It requires paying an additional tax of 25% (if filed within 12 months) or 50% (if filed within 24 months). It cannot be used to claim refunds or increase losses.

15. What is the difference between Form 26AS, AIS, and TIS?

Form 26AS is a statement showing tax deducted (TDS), tax collected (TCS), advance tax paid, and tax refunds. AIS (Annual Information Statement) captures all financial transactions (shares, mutual funds, interest, dividends, property, etc.). TIS (Taxpayer Information Summary) is a simplified version of AIS. You must reconcile your ITR figures with all three to avoid mismatch notices.
