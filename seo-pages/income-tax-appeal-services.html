CIT(A), ITAT and High Court representation

Appeals are formal remedies against assessment, penalty and demand orders. Timelines and evidence handling matter heavily.

Appeals dispute tax department orders formally

If additions, disallowances or penalties are wrong, taxpayers may appeal to CIT(A), then ITAT and higher courts. Each stage requires grounds, evidence, submissions and deadline discipline.

Service hub

Situations this page is built for

  • Assessment order with unexplained additions.
  • CIT(A) order received and ITAT appeal considered.
  • Ex-parte assessment without hearing.
  • Penalty order under tax provisions.
  • Stay of demand needed while appeal is pending.
  • Old demands remain despite prior appeal.
Service hub

Documents usually required

  • Assessment order with DIN.
  • Original ITR and computation.
  • Form 26AS/AIS.
  • Notice history and correspondence with AO.
  • Invoices, contracts, agreements or evidence for disputed item.
  • Previous appeal order if going to ITAT.
Service hub

Process

Read order

Identify additions and appealable points.

Draft grounds

Prepare concise legal and factual grounds.

File Form 35

CIT(A) appeal usually has strict timelines.

Apply for stay

Avoid recovery pressure while appeal is pending.

Attend hearing

Submit paper book and evidence.

Escalate if needed

Consider ITAT or court remedy after adverse order.

Service hub

Common mistakes

  • Missing 30-day appeal timeline.
  • Not applying for stay of demand.
  • Weak grounds without evidence or law.
  • Not submitting evidence at first appeal stage.
