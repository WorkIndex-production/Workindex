Info guide

What this covers

Gold taxation depends on the type of gold, holding period, purchase evidence and whether it was inherited or bought directly.

  • Physical gold, jewellery, coins and digital gold generally need capital-gains computation on sale.
  • Gold ETF and gold mutual fund holding periods may differ; verify whether the product is listed or unlisted.
  • Inherited gold is not taxed on receipt, but sale proceeds require cost and holding-period analysis.
  • CBDT jewellery seizure limits are not tax-free ownership limits; they only guide search proceedings.
  • GST on purchase does not eliminate capital gains tax on sale.

Use cases

Who this is for

  • Taxpayer selling family jewellery.
  • Investor redeeming gold ETF or gold mutual fund.
  • Person selling inherited gold without purchase invoices.
  • CA preparing ITR with gold capital gains.
Records

Documents and data to verify

  • Purchase invoice or valuation certificate.
  • Sale receipt and bank proof.
  • Inheritance documents, if relevant.
  • Fair market value support for pre-2001 gold.
Care points

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Treating wedding/inherited gold sale as tax-free.
  • Confusing seizure limits with exemption limits.
  • No evidence of original cost.
  • Wrongly using ETF rules for physical gold.
Action

How to proceed

  • Confirm the applicable financial year, assessment year, taxpayer type, state and portal status before acting.
  • Reconcile portal data with books, AIS/Form 26AS, GST returns, contracts, invoices, bank statements and source documents.
  • Prepare a written computation, checklist, filing note or response with assumptions clearly stated.
  • Download acknowledgements, challans, workings and evidence after filing or submission.
Questions people ask

FAQs

Is gold received as wedding gift taxable?

Receipt from specified relatives or on marriage may not be taxed, but sale of that gold can still trigger capital gains.

Can I use Section 54F for gold sale gains?

It may be possible if conditions are met. A CA should check house ownership, investment timing and the exact gain.

What if I have no invoice?

A valuation certificate and evidence of source/inheritance can help, but the computation should be documented carefully.
