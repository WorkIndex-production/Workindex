FAQ

Development — Common Questions

How should I structure payments with a developer? ▾
Always use milestone-based payments — never pay 100% upfront. A common structure is 30% on start, 40% on milestone completion, and 30% on final delivery and testing. WorkIndex recommends this to all clients.
Flutter vs React Native — which to choose for a mobile app? ▾
Both are cross-platform and cost-effective for Android + iOS from one codebase. Flutter has better performance; React Native has a larger community. Ask developers you receive quotes from — they'll recommend based on your specific app requirements.
What is an MVP and should I build one first? ▾
MVP (Minimum Viable Product) is a version with only core features. It saves 40–60% of initial budget, gets you to market faster, and lets you validate the idea before full investment. For new products, WorkIndex strongly recommends starting with an MVP.
Does the developer handle hosting and domain? ▾
Most developers will set up your hosting and domain as part of the project, though the recurring costs (hosting ₹3,000–₹10,000/year, domain ₹800/year) are paid by you directly. Clarify this in the scope before starting.

Before you request quotes

  • Entity type, owners/partners/directors, registrations already taken and filings missed.
  • PAN, Aadhaar/KYC, address proof, bank account, GST, TDS, payroll, contracts and previous filings.
  • Whether the work is one-time registration, recurring compliance, cleanup, advisory or notice response.
  • Deadline sensitivity and penalty exposure.
  • Whether accounting and tax filings need to be coordinated together.

Pricing context

Registration and compliance pricing varies by entity type, number of promoters, government fees, professional certification, catch-up filings and urgency.
