Workflow

Process

Confirm applicability

Identify the assessment year, financial year, state, form, registration status, taxpayer type, income head, notice section or transaction category before acting.

Reconcile source data

Match portal data with books, invoices, certificates, bank entries, AIS/Form 26AS, GSTR data, capital gains reports or payroll records.

Prepare the filing or response

Draft the return, registration, calculation, notice response, declaration, document checklist or project scope with supporting evidence.

Keep the audit trail

Download acknowledgements, challans, computation sheets, certificates, filings, orders, email approvals and expert working papers after completion.

Posting Brief

What to mention in your WorkIndex post

  • State the exact outcome you want: filing, registration, notice response, reconciliation, advisory note, monthly retainer, audit, certificate, model, website or one-time correction.
  • Mention period covered, current status, transaction count, employee count, number of GSTINs/entities, software used and whether a statutory deadline is running.
  • Add constraints: budget range, urgency, whether records are clean, whether portal access is available and whether expert must coordinate with vendors, banks, officers or staff.
  • For notices, include section, DIN, order date, response deadline and disputed amount. For city pages, include locality and whether in-person support is expected.
  • For ongoing work, ask for a monthly checklist, delivery date, escalation process and report format. This prevents vague quotes and makes comparison easier.
Quality Check

When expert help is worth it

Professional help becomes more valuable when the issue affects cash flow, refund, penalty, future financing, investor diligence, audit qualification, registration validity, employee records or customer invoices. Simple filings can sometimes be handled with basic support, but complex cases need someone who can explain the legal basis and defend the position later.

Before accepting a quote, ask what assumptions the expert is making and what would change the price. A transparent quote separates government fees, professional fees, data cleanup, follow-up, appeals and monthly maintenance.
