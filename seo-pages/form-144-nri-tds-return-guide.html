TDS/NRI guide

What this covers

Form 144 is a non-resident TDS return planning page for payments where residential status, DTAA, TRC, Form 10F and Section 195 withholding must be clear.

  • Form 144 is treated as a Batch 15 planning topic based on competitor-gap research and new-form discussions.
  • It is positioned as an NRI/non-resident TDS reporting topic connected with foreign payee withholding compliance.
  • The live portal form, acknowledgement format and certificate workflow should be checked before filing.
  • Keep payer/payee identity, amount, section, tax rate, challan and supporting document trails together.
Use cases

Who this is for

  • Buyer paying NRI seller.
  • Company paying foreign consultant.
  • Indian business remitting royalty or interest.
  • CA reconciling non-resident withholding.
Records

Documents and data to verify

  • Payee residency and PAN, if available.
  • TRC, Form 10F and DTAA note.
  • Agreement and invoice.
  • Challan and certificate evidence.
Care points

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using resident TDS rates for non-resident payees.
  • No TRC for DTAA benefit.
  • Ignoring capital gains withholding on NRI property sale.
  • Incomplete payee address/country data.
Action

How to proceed

  • Confirm the applicable financial year, assessment year, taxpayer type, state and portal status before acting.
  • Reconcile portal data with books, AIS/Form 26AS, GST returns, contracts, invoices, bank statements and source documents.
  • Prepare a written computation, checklist, filing note or response with assumptions clearly stated.
  • Download acknowledgements, challans, workings and evidence after filing or submission.
