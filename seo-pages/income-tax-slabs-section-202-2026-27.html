Fact-check notes

Last fact-checked: 2026-05-26

Transition rule: AY 2026-27 covers FY 2025-26 and continues under the Income-tax Act, 1961. The Income-tax Act 2025 Tax Year terminology applies for income earned from 1 April 2026 onward.

This page is written from official portal guidance and the supplied Batch 11 brief. Any future-dated form, rule or portal workflow should be verified in the active utility before filing or advising a client.

Calculator and guide

What this covers

Section 202 is the new Act slab reference for Tax Year 2026-27 planning. It should not be used for AY 2026-27 returns for FY 2025-26, which remain under the Income-tax Act, 1961.

  • Slabs used here: nil to Rs. 4,00,000; 5% from Rs. 4,00,001 to Rs. 8,00,000; 10% to Rs. 12,00,000; 15% to Rs. 16,00,000; 20% to Rs. 20,00,000; 25% to Rs. 24,00,000; 30% above Rs. 24,00,000.
  • Resident individual rebate is modelled only on normal slab income up to Rs. 12,00,000; special-rate income is kept separate.
  • Standard deduction and eligible new-regime deductions must be verified in the current utility before filing.

Care points

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using this page for AY 2026-27 return filing.
  • Applying rebate to special-rate income.
  • Ignoring surcharge or marginal relief for high income.
  • Confusing gross salary with taxable income.
