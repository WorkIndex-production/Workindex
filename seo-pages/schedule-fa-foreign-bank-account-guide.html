Fact check

Accuracy notes before you act

  • If a competitor page gives a fixed rate, penalty, date or exemption, verify it against the official source and your facts before copying it into a filing position.
  • RSUs and ESOPs are taxed twice: first as a salary perquisite under Section 17 on the exercise/vesting date (based on Fair Market Value minus exercise price), and second as capital gains when sold (LTCG at 12.5% if held >12 months for listed or >24 months for unlisted/foreign shares). For resident Indians, foreign shares (like US stocks/RSUs) must be disclosed in Schedule FA of the ITR, failing which a penalty of ₹10 lakh under the Black Money Act may apply.
  • Regulated finance, FEMA, securities and valuation work needs RBI, SEBI, AMFI or bank-source verification plus professional judgement on thresholds and documents.
