Posting Brief

What to mention in your WorkIndex post

  • State the exact outcome you want: filing, registration, notice response, reconciliation, advisory note, monthly retainer, audit, certificate, model, website or one-time correction.
  • Mention the period covered, current status, number of transactions or employees, number of GSTINs or entities, software used and whether any statutory deadline is already running.
  • Add the practical constraint: budget range, urgency, whether records are clean, whether portal access is available and whether you need the expert to coordinate with vendors, banks, officers or internal staff.
  • For notices and portal issues, paste the section, DIN, ARN, SRN, order date, response deadline and disputed amount. For city pages, include locality and whether in-person support is expected.
  • For ongoing work, ask for a monthly checklist, delivery date, escalation process and the format of reports you will receive. This prevents vague quotes and makes expert comparison much easier.
Quality Check

When expert help is worth it

Professional help becomes more valuable when the issue affects cash flow, tax credit, refund, penalties, future financing, investor diligence, audit qualification, government procurement, employee records or customer invoices. Simple filings can sometimes be handled with basic support, but complex cases need someone who can explain the legal basis, reconcile source data and defend the position later.

Before accepting a quote, ask what assumptions the expert is making and what would change the price. A transparent expert will separate government fees, professional fees, data cleanup, follow-up, appeals and monthly maintenance instead of folding everything into a vague package.
