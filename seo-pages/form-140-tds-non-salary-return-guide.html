TDS guide

What this covers

Form 140 is a non-salary TDS return planning topic for businesses deducting tax on vendor, contractor, professional, rent, interest and commission payments.

  • Form 140 is treated as a Batch 15 planning topic based on competitor-gap research and new-form discussions.
  • It is treated as a non-salary TDS return planning reference for vendor payment reporting.
  • The live portal form, acknowledgement format and certificate workflow should be checked before filing.
  • Keep payer/payee identity, amount, section, tax rate, challan and supporting document trails together.
Use cases

Who this is for

  • Company paying vendors under multiple TDS sections.
  • Finance team reconciling 194C, 194J, rent and commission deductions.
  • Business correcting PAN or challan mismatch.
  • CA filing quarterly non-salary TDS returns.
Records

Documents and data to verify

  • Vendor master and PAN validation.
  • Expense ledger section-wise mapping.
  • TDS challans and payment dates.
  • Lower deduction certificates and declarations.
Care points

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Wrong section mapping between contractor and professional fees.
  • Missing TDS on provisions or year-end entries.
  • No lower certificate validation.
  • Challan short payment after return filing.
Action

How to proceed

  • Confirm the applicable financial year, assessment year, taxpayer type, state and portal status before acting.
  • Reconcile portal data with books, AIS/Form 26AS, GST returns, contracts, invoices, bank statements and source documents.
  • Prepare a written computation, checklist, filing note or response with assumptions clearly stated.
  • Download acknowledgements, challans, workings and evidence after filing or submission.
