GST Registration for NGOs
Exempt charitable activities vs taxable fee income

NGOs and trusts can have exempt charitable receipts and taxable commercial activities at the same time. GST registration depends on the taxable side, not only NGO status.

Most charitable activities are exempt, specific services are not

Entities registered under charitable provisions may get exemptions for qualifying charitable activities. Fee-based training, hall rent, consultancy, publications, canteen or merchandise sales can still be taxable depending on facts.

Common mistakes

  • Registering when all income is genuinely exempt.
  • Not registering when training or rent income crosses threshold.
  • Claiming ITC on purchases used for exempt activities.
  • Assuming every FCRA grant is a taxable supply without contract review.

Questions People Ask

FAQs

Are donations taxable under GST?

Pure donations without supply are generally different from fees for services, but grant agreements should be reviewed.

What about FCRA grants from foreign donors?

FCRA status does not automatically decide GST. The underlying supply and conditions matter.

Does GST registration affect 12A status?

GST registration is separate, but accounting should keep charitable and taxable activities clear.
