Questions People Ask

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)? How is it taxed?

An HUF is a separate legal entity for tax purposes. It gets its own basic exemption limit (₹4,00,000 under New Regime) and standard tax deductions.

2. Which ITR form is used for business income and HUFs?

HUFs use ITR-2 or ITR-3. Individuals with business income use ITR-3 or ITR-4.

3. What is the new Section 43B(h) rule for MSME payments?

Expenses for purchases from registered MSMEs are allowed as deductions only if paid within 15 days (or 45 days if there is an agreement). Otherwise, the expense is disallowed in that FY.

4. How is rental income taxed in India?

Rental income is taxed under 'Income from House Property'. You get a standard deduction of 30% on the net annual value (rent minus municipal taxes).

5. What deductions can I claim for home loan interest?

Under Section 24(b), you can deduct up to ₹2,00,000 in interest for a self-occupied property (only under the Old Regime). Under the New Regime, interest deduction is not allowed for self-occupied properties.

6. Can I claim tax benefits for two self-occupied properties?

Yes, up to two properties can be declared as self-occupied (annual value is Nil). Additional properties are treated as deemed let-out and taxed.

7. What is the difference between ITR-3 and ITR-4?

ITR-3 is for businesses keeping books and claiming actual expenses. ITR-4 is for businesses/professionals using presumptive taxation (44AD/44ADA).

8. What is the due date for filing ITR for HUFs and businesses for FY 2025-26?

The due date is July 31, 2026 (for non-business/non-audit HUFs), August 31, 2026 (for non-audit businesses), and October 31, 2026 (for audit cases).

9. How is business depreciation calculated?

Depreciation is claimed on business assets (computers, building, machinery) under Section 32 based on block-of-assets tax depreciation rates.

10. Can business losses be carried forward?

Yes, business losses can be carried forward for 8 years and set off against business profits, provided the ITR is filed before the due date.

11. What happens if I miss the ITR filing deadline? What is the late filing fee?

If you file after the deadline (belated return under Section 139(4)), a late filing fee is charged under Section 234F: ₹5,000 if your total income exceeds ₹5,00,000, and ₹1,000 if your income is ₹5,00,000 or below. The final deadline to file a belated return for FY 2025-26 is December 31, 2026.

12. How can I track my Income Tax Refund status?

Log in to the e-filing portal and go to e-File > Income Tax Returns > View Filed Returns to check your status. Ensure your bank account is pre-validated on the e-filing portal and linked with Aadhaar/PAN to receive the refund directly.

13. How much time do I have to e-verify my ITR after filing?

For returns filed for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27), you must e-verify your ITR within 30 days of filing. If you fail to verify it via Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or physical signature (speed post to CPC Bengaluru) within 30 days, your return will be treated as invalid and not filed.

14. What is the difference between Form 26AS, AIS, and TIS?

Form 26AS is a statement showing tax deducted (TDS), tax collected (TCS), advance tax paid, and tax refunds. AIS (Annual Information Statement) captures all financial transactions (shares, mutual funds, interest, dividends, property, etc.). TIS (Taxpayer Information Summary) is a simplified version of AIS. You must reconcile your ITR figures with all three to avoid mismatch notices.

15. Can I file a revised return to correct mistakes in my ITR? What is the deadline?

Yes, under Section 139(5), you can file a revised return to correct any errors or omissions in your original filing. The deadline to file a revised return for FY 2025-26 is March 31, 2027.
