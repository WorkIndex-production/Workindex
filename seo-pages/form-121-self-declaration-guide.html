Help guide

What Form 121 is meant to solve

The new self-declaration framework aims to reduce duplicate 15G/15H style submissions and align deductors with income-tax portal data. Taxpayers should verify current portal availability and deductor instructions before relying on it.

Help guide

Who should review applicability

  • Senior citizens with FD interest.
  • Individuals with total income below taxable limit.
  • EPF withdrawal cases where TDS can apply.
  • Dividend or interest recipients with no final tax payable.
  • Taxpayers with multiple deductors.
Help guide

Process to expect

Estimate annual income

Include salary, pension, interest, rent, capital gains and other income.

Check tax liability

Declaration is only safe where conditions are met.

Submit declaration

Use the prescribed portal/deductor route.

Share reference

Give deductor acknowledgement/reference where required.

Help guide

Common mistakes

  • Assuming last year declaration continues.
  • Declaring below actual income.
  • Forgetting multiple banks/deductors.
  • Ignoring AIS/Form 26AS after TDS deduction.

AY 2026-27 means FY 2025-26 income under the Income-tax Act, 1961. Tax Year 2026-27 means FY 2026-27 income under the Income Tax Act, 2025. Do not mix the two labels.

Use official portal pages, CBDT notifications, the supplied Act PDF and ICAI material before making a filing, payroll, TDS/TCS or rebate decision.

Questions People Ask

FAQs

Which year should I use for Form 121 Self-Declaration?

Use AY 2026-27 for FY 2025-26 income under the Income-tax Act, 1961. Use Tax Year 2026-27 for FY 2026-27 income under the Income Tax Act, 2025.

What documents should I share with a tax expert?

Share the portal screenshot, exact year, income breakup, certificates, AIS/Form 26AS, notices, challans and any computation already prepared.

What to verify for Form 121 Self-Declaration

  • Exact income heads, including salary, house property, business/profession, capital gains, VDA and other sources.
