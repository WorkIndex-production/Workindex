Quick Answer

Revocation usually starts with compliance cleanup

GST cancellation often arises from non-filing, non-response, business closure, wrong details or officer action. Revocation generally needs pending returns, dues, reply and proper portal application where eligible.

Before filing revocation, confirm why registration was cancelled and whether pending returns, late fees, interest or documents must be fixed first.

Avoid Mistakes

Common red flags and mistakes

  • Filing revocation without clearing pending returns.
  • Ignoring tax, interest and late fee impact.
  • Using a generic reason instead of facts from the cancellation order.
  • Missing revocation timelines.
  • Continuing to issue invoices without checking GSTIN status.

Hiring Criteria

How to choose the right professional

  • Choose someone who asks for return data before quoting complex work.
  • Check experience with GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, 2B reconciliation and portal replies.
  • Ask whether late fee, interest and tax payment calculations are included.
  • For notices, confirm that the expert will prepare a point-wise reply with attachments.
  • For monthly retainers, clarify filing calendar and document collection process.
FAQs

Questions people ask before hiring

Can cancelled GST registration be restored?

In eligible cases, revocation or appeal routes may be available. Facts and timelines matter.

Do pending returns need to be filed first?

Usually, pending compliance must be cleaned up for non-filing cancellation cases.

How long does revocation take?

Simple cases can move in days, but officer review and pending compliance can extend timelines.
