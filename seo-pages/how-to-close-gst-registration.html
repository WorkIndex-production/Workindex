How-to

Cancellation is allowed when business stops or liability ceases

Voluntary cancellation can apply when business is discontinued, transferred, merged, below threshold, constitution changed or liability to register has ceased. Pending returns and notices can block or complicate cancellation.

How-to

Step-by-step cancellation process

File pending returns

Complete GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9 or other pending returns as applicable.

Pay dues

Clear tax, interest and late fees.

Reverse ITC

Review stock and capital goods ITC reversal where applicable.

File REG-16

Submit cancellation application with reason and effective date.

Track order

Officer may approve, query or reject.

File GSTR-10

Final return after cancellation is a common missed step.

How-to

GSTR-10 final return

GSTR-10 generally reports closing stock and ITC reversal after cancellation. Late filing can attract fees, so diarise the due date after order.

How-to

Common mistakes

  • Not filing GSTR-10 after cancellation.
  • Cancelling with open ITC balance.
  • Applying while monthly returns are pending.
  • Ignoring pending notices before cancellation.
How-to

When a CA is needed

  • Large ITC balance.
  • Pending GST notice or demand.
  • Business transfer or merger.
  • Officer-cancelled GSTIN restoration.
